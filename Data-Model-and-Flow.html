1 · Data model — Scheduling & Master Data

Schedule → Day → Anaesthetist → List is a fixed canvas (two Lists per anaesthetist per Day, rolling 4-month horizon). Status, Hospital, Surgeon and Cards are "painted onto" it. Master data is decoupled and referenced by ID.

  • The List is the unit of availability and of billing approval — availability varies independently between AM and PM.
  • Card and Procedure carry full audit history — lastModifiedBy/At plus an append-only log on both entities (RFP Design Principle 10: billing route, governing Contract and overrides must all be historically reproducible). Contracts are effective-dated and billing lines store a computed snapshot amount, so an invoice re-renders against the rules in force when it was raised even after a Contract changes.
  • A List can be reassigned to another anaesthetist at short notice without disturbing its Cards, status or audit trail.
  • governingContract resolves for the Hospital and Insurer routes — every Hospital and direct-billing Insurer holds a default Type 1 Contract, so there is no "no contract found" branch on those routes. The Billable Party route has no Contract by default (standard rate + optional override); a Contract is attached only in the special case where one is set up to permit an individually-arranged structure (e.g. an hourly rate). Hence governingContractId is nullable.
  • Overrides and agreed pricing are typed, not a bare number — overrideType distinguishes fixed fee / $ adjustment / % adjustment (RFP: "both agreed fixed fees and $ or % adjustments"). CONTRACT_PRICE holds agreed price lists keyed by procedure-type (RVG base code), Surgeon and/or procedure ordinal (2nd-procedure rules). Contracts may be held by a Hospital, Insurer or Surgeon/group (bariatric, TMJ, ACC).
  • PERMANENT_LIST is the template; canvas LIST rows are generated instances. Availability/holiday calendars are reconciled against the canvas, not merged in.

2 · Data model — Billing Engine & Xero

The Billing Engine references Card/Procedure by ID. Billing destination is resolved per Procedure, so one Card can yield multiple invoices — each creating a matched Xero ACCREC + draft ACCPAY pair, linked by GUID.

    PROCEDURE ||--o{ INVOICE_LINE : "billed on"

    BILLING_CASE ||--|| XERO_ACCREC : "links via GUID"
    BILLING_CASE ||--|| XERO_ACCPAY : "links via GUID"

  • InvoiceNumber is the reconciliation key (quoted on remittances); Reference/CaseReference is display-only. AA prefers ACCREC/ACCPAY numbers to be similar for human matching.
  • Two-state money model: invoice paid (into AA) and disbursed to anaesthetist are separate states — AA operates like a trust account. Direct-to-anaesthetist hospital payments are retired.
  • Payment detection: Xero webhook (primary) + daily poll (safety net), idempotent by InvoiceID. Paid ACCREC flips ACCPAY DRAFT → AUTHORISED. Partials pass through pro-rata.
  • Bulk remittance & bank rec stay in Xero — out of scope beyond reliable InvoiceNumber.

2a · Patient identity & Xero contact lifecycle

Three identifiers, each scoped to one system, keep Xero performant (~10k active-contact soft limit vs ~28k invoices/yr, ~99% one-time) while the NHI never leaves the practice system.

3 · List lifecycle (the billing trigger)

Approval state lives on the List, not the Card. Reaching AUTHORISED locks the Cards and hands the whole List to the Billing Engine as one unit.

stateDiagram-v2
    [*] --> DRAFT
    DRAFT: DRAFT - fully editable by anaesthetist, also updatable by office and integrations
    SUBMITTED: SUBMITTED - editable by office only (OfficeAdmin), anaesthetist loses edit access
    AUTHORISED: AUTHORISED - Cards locked, immutable

    DRAFT --> DRAFT: anaesthetist edits Cards
    DRAFT --> SUBMITTED: taps Completed, all Cards valid
    SUBMITTED --> SUBMITTED: office corrections (issues resolved by phone)
    SUBMITTED --> AUTHORISED: office signs off after sanity check
    AUTHORISED --> [*]: handed to Billing Engine as a unit
  • No "Returned" state — Cards are never sent back to the anaesthetist; office resolves issues by phone.
  • The List drops off the mobile view at invoice generation (proposed trigger, to confirm — not merely at AUTHORISED). The List itself does not reappear; its resulting invoice line items (ACCPAY) surface next day in the flat outstanding-balance view.

Narrative walk-through

  1. Origination — a List is reserved for a surgeon at a hospital, from a Permanent List or ad-hoc by phone. (The ~80% figure in the RFP is the share of Surgeon assignments defined by the Permanent List vs. assigned manually by office staff — not a List-origination split.)
  2. Card population — Cards fill over weeks via hospital integration (HL7 v2 → FHIR; target FHIR-native, near real-time), emailed PDF lists (read/edit/ingest — OCR only implied for the photo path), or ad-hoc mobile entry (incl. photo, processed). Cards stay mutable to the day.
  3. Pre-payment (if required) — for a self-funded patient flagged "full/split pre-payment required", a pre-procedure invoice is raised and payment collected before surgery; any balance follows the normal post-procedure path. (Note the timing tension with the AUTHORISED-triggered billing below — see §5.)
  4. Data capture — on the day, BTM data per Procedure (base RVG code, start/handover times, modifiers; ASA seeds the modifier field).
  5. Submit → Authorise — anaesthetist marks List SUBMITTED (app enforces minimum data); office sanity-checks and marks AUTHORISED, locking Cards.
  6. Billing Engine — resolves counterparty per Procedure; for Hospital/Insurer looks up the governing Contract (Type 1/2/3), for Billable Party applies the standard rate + optional override (no Contract unless one permits an individual arrangement). Computes the fee by charge basis — RVG/BTM (T1/T2 breakpoint at 2 hrs, base capped at one), agreed fixed fee, or rate×time. Enforces both split-billing forms — multiple procedures (2nd+ = time only) and one procedure across two funders (per-funder billing lines → two invoices) — then groups by counterparty.
  7. Invoicing + Xero — invoices generated and emailed from the Billing Engine (supports the GST agency relationship); matched ACCREC + draft ACCPAY created in Xero, linked by GUID. The List drops off mobile; its invoice line items surface next day in outstanding balances.
  8. Payment + disbursement — payment lands in AA; webhook + daily poll flips ACCPAY to AUTHORISED; payables run disburses to the anaesthetist. "Paid" and "disbursed" tracked separately.
  9. Post-operative charges — pain consults, HDU/ward reviews, nerve catheters etc. can arise days later. Because the original Card is immutable once locked, these are captured as a new Card/addendum (cardType = postOpAddendum) that runs its own authorise → bill cycle. (Mechanism to confirm — see §5.)

5 · Design tensions / open questions to resolve in discovery

  • NHI in Xero — RFP contradiction (see 2a): Appendix 1 (searchable cross-reference field on the Xero contact) vs. Appendix 2 (never in Xero). Model follows Appendix 2; needs a ruling.
  • Billing trigger vs pre-payment — RFP contradiction: billing is triggered at AUTHORISED (after the procedure), yet self-funded pre-payment must be collected before surgery. Confirm how/when the pre-procedure invoice is raised relative to the AUTHORISED handoff.
  • Immutable Cards vs post-op charges — RFP contradiction: Cards lock at AUTHORISED and hand off as a unit, but post-op charges arise days later. Confirm the addendum / new-Card mechanism and how it re-bills.
  • One route per Procedure vs two-funder split — RFP contradiction: "each Procedure resolves to exactly one route" vs. a single procedure split across two funders (two invoices). Modelled here via a per-line funder; confirm intent.
  • Billable Party Contract — RFP contradiction: "no Contract applies" to the Billable Party route vs. Method 3 requiring the Billable Party to hold a Contract permitting an hourly arrangement. Modelled as a nullable Contract; confirm.
  • Permanent List surgeon: the narrative implies the Permanent List defines the surgeon (~80%), but the RFP field list omits it. Added here as nullable surgeonId; confirm.
  • Billable-party (guardian) identity: billablePartyId references Patient by NHI, but a guardian paying for a minor may not be a patient — confirm how non-patient payers are identified/keyed.
  • Concurrency — a Card/Procedure edited simultaneously by integration + mobile app.
  • List reassignment mechanism — preserving Cards, status and audit history.
  • Availability vs holiday reconciliation — hard constraint, soft warning, or advisory?
  • Card- vs List-level billing failure — does one failed Card block the whole List?
  • Where office billing-flow monitoring lives — Admin Web App vs dedicated Billing Engine admin surface.
  • Exact "List disappears from mobile" trigger — proposed as invoice generation, to be confirmed and locked down.
  • Hospital route non-payment — fallback to the Billable Party, or is the Hospital route final?
  • Insurer route scaling — whether direct-insurer billing needs its own rate structure.
  • Second-procedure & group pricing rules — CONTRACT_PRICE supports keys by procedure-type/surgeon/ordinal, but group-held contracts (e.g. COS) may need a first-class Group entity; capture detailed rules.
  • NHI dual-format (Appendix 1) — support both old (mod-24) and new (mod-23, alphanumeric) by 1 July 2027.
