From theatre schedule to paid anaesthetist.

Anaesthesia Associates coordinates bookings and billing for about 85 independent anaesthetists. This guide shows who uses the prototype, how work moves between them, what to click in the demo, and where to be honest about work still in progress.

01 · What the app is for

One connected operating and billing workflow.

The prototype is not a roster marketplace. It keeps a changing theatre schedule, captures the inputs that determine fees, controls who can edit, and follows the money from payer to anaesthetist.

1 Schedule AA maintains AM/PM Lists across the roster.
→
2 Capture Anaesthetists complete Cards and BTM inputs.
→
3 Control Office reviews, corrects and authorises the List.
→
4 Bill The Billing Engine rates and issues invoices.
→
5 Settle Target state: AA collects, then separately pays the anaesthetist.
!
The key correction to remember

The RFP contains no “SSA” and no self-service shift claiming. The user is an anaesthetist; the scheduling unit is a half-day List. Anaesthetists maintain availability and help identify cover, while the AA office assigns or reassigns Lists. The prototype’s request/offer controls are a proposed interaction, not an RFP-mandated acceptance workflow.

          05 · The control model

          Three states decide who may edit.

          Operational status describes what a List is used for. Approval state controls mutability. They are separate concepts.

          What the half-day session is being used for.

          Private Public Pre-op assessment Holiday Unavailable Free
          DRAFT

          Anaesthetist, office and mapped integrations may update.

          SUBMITTED

          Anaesthetist is read-only. Office checks and may correct.

          AUTHORISED

                07 · Presenter cheat sheet

                The words and rules worth memorising.

                Start with the five nouns. Then remember the lifecycle, the Billing Engine’s role and the difference between money collected and money disbursed.

                The five nouns

                Schedule The rolling four-month calendar that holds every anaesthetist’s AM and PM Lists.

                Strong phrases to use

                • “The List is the unit of availability and approval.”
                • “The fixed canvas exists even when there is no activity.”
                • “Status, hospital, surgeon and Cards are painted onto the canvas.”
                • “The office handles exceptions; the system removes repetitive work.”
                • “The captured inputs remain reproducible, not just the final total.”
                • “This is a candidate design we would validate during discovery.”

                Statements to avoid

                • “Anaesthetists pick up open shifts.”
                • “The app automatically chooses the clinical RVG code.”
                • “ACC has its own billing route.”
                • “Every insured patient is billed to an insurer.”
                • “Xero calculates the invoice.”
                • “The prototype is connected to live hospital or payment services.”
                The core calculation
                Fee = (Base + Time + Modifiers) × Dr Souter’s own unit value

                The anaesthetist chooses the RVG code. The system validates and calculates; it does not infer clinical judgement.

                Time units get faster after two hours

                First 2 hours1 unit per 15 minutes
                4 units per hour
                After 2 hours1 unit per 10 minutes
                6 units per hour

                Partial-interval rounding is not settled by the RFP; the prototype rounds up per started interval as a discovery assumption.

                Three explicit billing routes — per Procedure

                1 Hospital / contract holder Includes ACC-related work. ACC is context, not a fourth route.
                2 Billable Party Usually the patient or guardian, including reimbursement-style insurance.
                3 Direct-claim Insurer Only where the insurer accepts a direct claim from AA.

                Additional Procedure

                Time-only. Do not repeat Base or Modifier units in the same episode.

                Two funders

                Conserved billing lines may be allocated across funders; the prototype groups by counterparty.

                Contracts

                Type 1 standard/reference, Type 2 agreed/discount, Type 3 fixed price.

                Payerhospital, insurer or patient

                ACCPAY → Anaesthetistmoney separately disbursed

                Paid into AA and disbursed to the anaesthetist are separate states. Partial receipts authorise proportional payment. Anaesthetist apps read the Billing Engine mirror, never Xero directly.

                Likely evaluator questions

                Who uses mobile versus web?
                The same anaesthetist. Mobile is the in-theatre/on-the-move interface; web is the wider planning and accounts view.
                Who creates the “shifts”?
                The system creates the AM/PM List canvas. Permanent Lists provide recurring defaults. Office staff reserve and amend Lists and handle exceptions. “Shift” is not the RFP term.
                Can Dr Souter edit after submission?
                No. SUBMITTED Cards are read-only to her and editable only by office. AUTHORISED Cards are immutable.
                What happens when office finds a mistake?
                Office phones for clarification and makes the correction. There is no Returned state.
                Why is Xero not doing all the billing?
                AA has Procedure-level rating, grouping and agency rules. The Billing Engine calculates and issues invoices; Xero is the AR and banking add-on.
                Is the demo using live patient or integration data?
                No. All names and data are fictional. OCR/NHI and invoice email are simulated in-browser. Hospital feeds, Xero and payment simulations are planned for later phases and are not yet working demo flows.

                Discovery decisions — do not overclaim

                NHI in Xero is contradictory
                One RFP section allows a searchable Xero cross-reference; Appendix 2 says NHI never leaves the practice system. The prototype chooses the stricter no-NHI-in-Xero reading.
                Exact List disappearance trigger
                The RFP is internally tense between AUTHORISED and invoice generation. The prototype removes the List after the billing run stamps billedAt.
                Card failure isolation and monitor location
                The prototype plans to isolate a failed Card and place monitoring in Admin Web. Both are design proposals to confirm.
                Reassignment and availability mechanics
                Free-target, absorb and regenerate preserves the fixed canvas. Availability and holiday clashes are advisory flags. The RFP leaves both mechanics open.
                Split billing and pre-payment
                Different counterparties receive separate invoices. The prototype’s per-line funder allocation is its chosen representation for the RFP’s internally tense route/split model. Pre-payment timing and an office override are planned interpretations.
                Concurrency, modifiers and rounding
                Production update conflicts remain a discovery topic. Demo modifier values are not a certified NZSA table, and partial time rounding is a prototype assumption.
