AnaesthesiaAssociates Ltd Booking & Billing · Master Demo Guide
Prototype snapshot · 24 July 2026

The prototype in one page

A fully interactive React front end over a fake in-browser backend that demonstrates every major feature of the RFP's candidate architecture. It is a demo prototype, not production software: all data is fictional and every external system is simulated.

The 30-second explanation. Anaesthesia Associates coordinates bookings and billing for about 85 independent anaesthetists. The system maintains a rolling AM/PM schedule, keeps patient Cards current, lets anaesthetists capture BTM billing data, lets the office check and authorise completed Lists, then calculates and issues invoices. Xero handles receivables and banking, while the Billing Engine stays the system that translates financial events back into each anaesthetist's view.

The mental model

Schedule
  → Day
    → Anaesthetist
      → AM List and PM List
        → Card (one patient appointment)
          → Procedure(s)
            → Billing line(s)
  • The system creates a rolling four-month canvas with exactly two Lists, AM and PM, for every active anaesthetist every day.
  • Permanent arrangements, availability, hospital, surgeon, status and Cards are painted onto it.
  • A List is not an employment shift, and a Card is not a shift application.
  • The anaesthetist completes Cards and submits the whole List; the office checks and authorises it; authorisation locks the Cards and hands the List to the Billing Engine.

The five nouns

TermMeaning
ScheduleThe rolling four-month calendar
ListOne anaesthetist's half-day AM or PM operating session
CardOne patient appointment inside a List
ProcedureOne clinical procedure inside a Card; each resolves its own billing route
BTMBase units + Time units + Modifier units

The lifecycle

DRAFT → SUBMITTED → AUTHORISED → Billing run completed
  • DRAFT: anaesthetist, office and integrations may update.
  • SUBMITTED: office-only edits; the anaesthetist sees it completed and unbilled.
  • AUTHORISED: Cards are locked; the whole List hands to billing.
  • Billing-run completion stamps billedAt and removes the List from the anaesthetist's work view. There is no RETURNED state.

Who uses each app

Three user-facing apps, two personas. Mobile and web are two contexts for the same anaesthetist role, not two jobs. The Admin app covers several office responsibilities under one persona.

Dr Melanie Souter · anaesthetist (mobile-first)

May: edit own DRAFT Cards, submit when every active Card is complete, see colleagues' session availability (not their patient details). May not: edit after submission, authorise, or touch master data.

Primary persona 2

Dr Souter at a desktop · anaesthetist web

A usage context, not a separate role. Wider view of upcoming work, availability and finances, with the same List, Card and BTM actions.

Balances and GST activity are now live over the Billing Engine's mirror. Use web for overview and planning; use mobile for the procedure-day action.

Primary persona 3

Kirsty W. · scheduling coordinator

Office. Needs the whole day across the roster. Coordinates standing arrangements, ad-hoc bookings, hospital changes, cancellations and short-notice cover.

The one-day dashboard is the control tower: move one Card for a routine reschedule, reassign a whole List for illness cover, preserving Cards and audit through every change.

Primary persona 4

Kirsty W. · billing review and authorisation

May be the same staff member. Reviews all Cards of a submitted List as a set, corrects office-owned data without returning the List, authorises a clean List, and monitors later billing, Xero and integration exceptions.

Critical rule: there is no RETURNED state. The office phones for clarification and corrects it; the anaesthetist does not reopen the Card.

External actors (not login personas)

Surgeon rooms and hospital/PAS supply booking information; patients or guardians supply billing details and may receive an invoice; a direct-claim insurer may receive an invoice through its upload workflow; the Billing Engine calculates and issues; Xero provides AR and banking. The business process end to end

One audited flow from a changing theatre schedule to captured clinical inputs, controlled office authorisation, reliable invoicing, and transparent collection and disbursement.

Surgeon/hospital → office     standing arrangement, phone, PDF or hospital update
Office → schedule             reserve/update List and Cards
Schedule → anaesthetist       show assigned List and patient Cards
Anaesthetist                    capture procedure and BTM data
Anaesthetist → office         submit completed List
Office                          sanity-check and correct Cards
Office → Billing Engine       authorise whole List
Billing Engine                  resolve route, Contract, fee, invoice grouping

Xero → Billing Engine         payment webhook or reconciliation result

The two kinds of state. Do not confuse operational List status (Private, Public, Pre-op, Holiday, Unavailable, Free, what the session is used for) with the approval lifecycle (DRAFT, SUBMITTED, AUTHORISED, who may edit it).

The workflows

0 · Create and maintain the schedule canvas

The system ensures every active anaesthetist has two Lists a day across the rolling horizon; Permanent Lists project standing arrangements; availability and hospital holidays are reconciled; conflicts surface without deleting the List. Ready

1 · Reserve a List and populate patient Cards

Triggers: a Permanent List, a surgeon's call, a hospital message, an emailed PDF, or a missing Card found on the day. Manual fallbacks are first-class: phone advice (office), mobile/web manual Card, photo extraction, and PDF review-and-ingest (deduped by NHI). Every change writes lastModifiedBy/At and append-only audit. Ready

2 · Plan the day and manage late changes

The office reads the status-coloured grid, moves one Card for a routine reschedule, edits hospital, surgeon or actual times, soft-cancels with a reason (Cards stay visible, excluded from billing), and adds internal day notes. Ready

3 · Find cover and reassign a whole List

The anaesthetist offers a Free session or requests cover; the office views Free anaesthetists, reassigns the whole List (Cards and history), the target's empty Free List is absorbed, and a fresh List regenerates in the vacated slot. Free-target, absorb and regenerate is the prototype's proposed reading of an open RFP mechanism. Ready

4 · Capture BTM and complete a Card

Per Procedure: select the RVG base code, record start and handover, and the system computes tiered Time units (1/15 min for the first two hours, then 1/10 min), plus ASA and modifiers, at the anaesthetist's own value per unit. A positioning modifier is blocked if the base code already absorbs it; an additional Procedure is time-only. When every active Card is complete, the List is submittable. Ready

5 · Submit the whole List and office review

The anaesthetist marks the List complete and submits; it becomes SUBMITTED and read-only to her. The office reviews the full set (route, Contract, Insurer, reference, BTM, advisory flags), corrects office-owned data, optionally logs a phone note, and authorises. One list-authorised event hands the whole List to the Billing Engine. Review is a human sanity check, not an automatic gate. Ready

6 · Calculate and issue invoices

The Billing Engine resolves the explicit route per Procedure (hospital/contract holder, billable party, direct-claim insurer), selects the governing Contract and rating method, calculates from the captured snapshot, enforces time-only additional Procedures and conserved two-funder allocations, groups by counterparty, and produces contract-holder or patient invoice layouts. Billing-run completion stamps billedAt and removes the List from the anaesthetist's views. Ready

7 · Collect money and disburse to the anaesthetist

The Billing Engine creates a Xero pair: ACCREC (what the payer owes AA) and a DRAFT ACCPAY (what AA owes the anaesthetist). A webhook (with a daily reconciliation poll as safety net) marks payment; the payable authorises, proportionally for a partial; the payables run disburses. Paid into AA and disbursed to the anaesthetist are separate states. The apps read the Billing Engine's mirror, never Xero directly. Ready

8 · Failures, late additions and compliance

Pre-payment: a visible completion block until paid, lifted only by an audited office override. Post-op: a linked addendum Card with its own cycle; the original stays immutable. Billing failure: the failed Card is isolated, others invoice, then resolve-and-retry. Integration failure: a malformed message retries then dead-letters for manual intervention; a duplicate is an idempotent no-op; a message for a SUBMITTED/AUTHORISED target is parked. Compliance: everything is audited, both NHI formats validate, and NHI never enters Xero (a flagged reading of contradictory RFP appendices). Ready

Cheat sheet

The rules most likely to be questioned, and the phrasing that keeps the demo faithful to the RFP.

Fee calculation

Fee = (Base + Time + Modifier units) × anaesthetist's own dollar value per unit
  • Time: 1 unit per 15 min for the first two hours, then 1 unit per 10 min.
  • Each anaesthetist has an independent value per unit.
  • The anaesthetist selects the RVG code; the system does not infer it. Some codes are ranges.
  • Positioning cannot be charged again if the base code already includes it.
  • Partial-interval rounding is a prototype assumption (round up per started interval).

Three billing routes

  1. Hospital or other contract holder
  2. Billable Party, usually the patient
  3. Direct-claim Insurer

Explicit per Procedure, not inferred from flags.

Patient insurance distinction

Direct Insurer route: AA invoices the one insurer that accepts direct claims. Insured reimbursement: AA invoices the patient, who claims from their insurer. Not the same workflow.

Split billing

An additional Procedure in the same episode is time-only; Base and Modifiers are not charged again. One Procedure may allocate conserved lines across two funders. Group by counterparty; different funders create separate invoices.

Contracts

Type 1 reference only (standard rate); Type 2 agreed rate/discount; Type 3 fixed price. Every hospital and direct-billing insurer has a protected default Type 1. Surgeon, group and organisation holders do not have that guaranteed fallback in the prototype.

The money model

Payer → ACCREC → AA account → ACCPAY → Anaesthetist
  • ACCREC is money AA is collecting; ACCPAY is money AA owes the anaesthetist.
  • Paid into AA and disbursed to the anaesthetist are separate states.
  • Partial payments authorise proportional disbursement.
  • The apps read the Billing Engine's mirror, never Xero directly.
  • Outstanding balances are flat individual payable rows, not a Card-level rollup and not amounts the anaesthetist personally owes.

RFP ambiguities and discovery decisions

Where the RFP is open or internally inconsistent, the prototype picks a reading and labels it. Present these as design proposals to confirm with AA, not settled facts.

1 · NHI in Xero

One section allows NHI as a searchable Xero cross-reference; Appendix 2 says NHI never leaves the practice system. The prototype takes the stricter no-NHI-in-Xero reading.

2 · List disappearance trigger

A table implies disappearance at AUTHORISED; the text says invoice generation is the precise trigger and needs confirming. The prototype uses billing-run completion.

3 · Billing failure isolation

The RFP asks whether one failed Card blocks the whole List. The prototype isolates the failed Card and bills the others.

4 · Monitoring location

Admin Web vs a separate Billing Engine screen is left open. The prototype places monitoring in Admin Web.

5 · List reassignment mechanics

Reassignment is required; the mechanism is open. Free-target, absorb target, regenerate vacated slot is the prototype's proposal.

6 · Availability and holiday conflicts

Hard constraints or warnings is left open. The prototype uses advisory conflict flags.

7 · Split-billing invoice count

Grouping text says same-counterparty Procedures share an invoice; split-billing text says two invoices. The prototype groups by counterparty, two invoices when funders differ.

8 · Pre-payment

Payment before the procedure is required; the fit with the normal authorised-List trigger and any override is unspecified. The prototype uses a visible completion block and a reasoned office override.

9 · Concurrency

Several sources can change a Card. The prototype is single-user with audited last-write-wins; production concurrency is a discovery topic, shown via the audit trail and live integration updates.

10 · Modifier values and time rounding

The RFP names example ranges, not a certified NZSA table. The prototype values are demo-plausible; partial-interval rounding is an assumption. Neither is authoritative.

Likely evaluator questions

Who actually uses mobile versus web?

The same anaesthetist. Mobile is the in-theatre/on-the-move interface; web is the wider planning and accounts view.

Who creates the shifts?

The system creates AM and PM Lists on the rolling canvas; Permanent Lists supply recurring defaults; the office reserves, amends and handles exceptions. "Shift" is not the RFP term.

Can an anaesthetist edit after submission?

No. Submitted Cards are read-only to the anaesthetist and editable only by office; authorised Cards are immutable.

What happens when the office finds a mistake?

Office staff phone for clarification and make the correction. There is no Returned state.

Why is Xero not doing all the billing?

AA has agency-specific billing rules and needs Procedure-level calculation, grouping and anaesthetist-facing views. Xero is the AR and banking add-on.

How do late hospital changes work?

While the List is DRAFT, mapped hospital messages update the Card in near real time. Once SUBMITTED or AUTHORISED, an inbound change parks for manual intervention.

What if the patient has no NHI?

The RFP is internally inconsistent (unique identifier vs "where available"). The prototype uses a hidden internal ID as the invariant and allows a provisional NHI-pending patient.

Is the demo using real patient or integration data?

No. All names and data are fictional; hospital, PDF/OCR, NHI, email, Xero and payment events are simulated in-browser.
